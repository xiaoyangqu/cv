Research experience during PhD

Development of methods for predicting protein-ligand interactions:
  • Polarization force field-based method
    • • Construction of protein residue-ligand interaction energy (quantum mechanical level) datasets using a chunking approach.
    • Introduction of an electrostatic intrusion model to correct the AMOEBA polarization force field (i.e., the AMOEBA_CP force field that takes into account quantum effects at close range)..
    • Re-parameterizing the force field using the constructed quantum mechanical energy data set.
    • Combined with multi-step simulated annealing (molecular dynamics approach) to successfully predict the change in inhibitor binding affinity by 144 amino acid mutations in the cancer target Abl kinase.


  • Machine learning-based method
    • • The development of new machine learning descriptors based on water molecule networks at protein-ligand binding interfaces and the interaction of water and complexes.
    • Systematic enhancement of multiple classical machine learning computational methods using the new descriptors.
    • New features combined with models trained under different algorithms are able to reliably predict interactions, predict molecular binding conformations as well as screen bindable molecules.
